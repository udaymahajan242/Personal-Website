My Education.

I was born in a middle-class family in the Republic of India. My family made a commitment to do everything within their power to instill in me an assimilation of reverence for learning and the significance of being self-sufficient and diligent. Therefore, I did my schooling in boarding schools. I studied from grade 4- to grade 10 at the esteemed Pinegrove School where I maintained an exceptional academic record while studying strenuous subjects. I also developed strong leadership skills by leading the Music and Information Technology Clubs and learned to collaborate with a wide variety of people by participating in various types of games and extracurricular activities such as cricket, soccer, yoga, cross country, athletics, music performances, swimming, and community service. 

University of Washington, Seattle

Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science and Engineering

The University of Washington, commonly referred to as simply Washington, UW, or informally U-Dub, is a public flagship research university in Seattle, Washington, United States. Founded in 1861, Washington is one of the oldest universities on the West Coast.

Washington is a member of the Association of American Universities and is consistently ranked among the top 15 universities in the world by a variety of international publications.

The Motto(s) of UW is "Lux sit" (Latin), meaning
Let there be light and Be Boundless.

Previously known as University of Washington's Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) Department, Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering is consistently ranked among the top ten programs in the United States of America.

The Allen School educates tomorrow’s innovators and engages in research that advances core and emerging areas of the field. The school also leads a broad range of multi-disciplinary initiatives that demonstrate the transformative power of computing and are nationally recognized for its success in promoting diversity.

Pierce College, Lakewood

Pierce College is a community college district operating in and serving Pierce County in the U.S. state of Washington. The district consists of two main colleges, Pierce College Fort Steilacoom in Lakewood and Pierce College Puyallup in Puyallup, and auxiliary campuses at Fort Lewis, McChord Air Force Base, and in South Hill. The college district is home to 16,916 students.

Pierce College creates quality educational opportunities for a diverse community of learners to thrive in an evolving world.

St. Stephen's School, Togan (Chandigarh)

St. Stephen’s School is a Roman Catholic, English Medium, Co-educational Institution, with reputation of developing an all-round ability of the students with a balanced training that leads to a proper appreciation of academic, athletic, ethical and moral excellence.

The school is located in Togan near Chandigarh, India and was founded in 1982. The school is affiliated to the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) board.

Motto of St. Stephen's School: “Semper Sursum", meaning Always Aim Higher.

Pinegrove School, Solan.

Pinegrove School, established in 1991, is a Co-educational, purely residential, English medium public school affiliated to the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE), Delhi, up to 10th and 12th Grades. 

The School is situated in two locations - Dharampur(Estd. 1991) and Subathu (Estd. 1998), both in the most picturesque scenery of the Shimla Hills, in District Solan of Himachal Pradesh, India.

Pinegrove is reputed for its high standards in value education, good work-ethics and discipline, while maintaining the highest standards of academic and sporting excellence. 

It is a member of the prestigious IPSC (Indian Public School’s Conference). The school is also member of the British Council sponsored UKIERI (UK-India Educational Research Initiative) and GSP (Global School Programme), Round Square Conference, the NCC (National Cadet Corps), the IAYP (International Awards for Young People) which was the erstwhile Duke of Edinburgh’s Award Program and is ISO 9001:2008 certified.

Motto of Pinegrove School: "By Toil To Stars", meaning aiming to reach the stars of achievement.
