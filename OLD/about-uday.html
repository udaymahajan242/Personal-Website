I was born in a middle-class family in the Republic of India. My family made a commitment to do everything within their power to instill in me an assimilation of reverence for learning and the significance of being self-sufficient and diligent. Therefore, I lived in boarding schools, from grades 4-12, where I maintained an exceptional academic record while studying strenuous subjects. I also developed strong leadership skills by leading the Music and Information Technology Clubs and learned to collaborate with a wide variety of people by participating in various types of games and extracurricular activities such as cricket, soccer, yoga, cross country, athletics, music performances, swimming, and community service. There were certain failures and certain accomplishments; I am glad that I made both. The army style schedule at boarding schools inculcated me with surviving difficult changes and personal adversity, being consistently disciplined, understanding an issue from different perspectives, managing finances, keeping physically fit, and making effective and quick decisions as a responsible citizen of the global society. I know that these traits will continue to serve me throughout my life.
​
After immigrating to the United States in late 2014, I began attending Pierce College in early 2015. My dedication and grit towards my educational goals and involvement outside academics were acknowledged by American Honors and the Phi Theta Kappa honor society at Pierce College. I like to push myself out of my comfort zone, maximize my learning and add value to the world around me.
Currently, I’m a junior at the University of Washington’s Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science and Engineering majoring in Computer Engineering. I believe that computers and electricity have led to outstanding innovations ranging from smartphones to self-driving cars. With the world becoming increasingly dependent on technology, the advancement of computers and electricity will be key for future progress and innovations. My goal as a professional is to design and create worthwhile electrical circuits, computer hardware and programs for the betterment of the world.
